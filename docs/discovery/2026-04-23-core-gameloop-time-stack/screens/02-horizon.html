Meaning over weeks — at what horizon?

Today the smallest unit above "day" is basically nothing — an achievement unlock here, a streak number there. Pick the horizon you want to feel inhabited. (Multi-select OK, but be greedy with one.)

W

Week — the Sunday recap

"This week with Ronki" — a small ritual at week's end. Wert der Woche, weekly digest, a moment to look back. Closest in time, easiest to feel, possible to ship in days.

M

Month — chapter close

End of April: a "chapter" page in the Buch, a Ronki reflection, maybe a small visual change to the Lagerfeuer. Long enough to feel like accumulation, short enough to come around.

S

Season — quarterly arc

Spring → Summer → Autumn → Winter. Lagerfeuer scenery shifts, Freunde rotate, the world quietly changes around the kid. Slow, painterly, season-of-life.

J

The whole journey — fade-out arc

The Wave 3 thing. Months from now, Louis has built his rituals, Ronki authors a goodbye, the app steps aside. The keepsake (Jahresalbum) is the artefact left behind.

∞

All of them — there's a missing time-stack

The structural answer: today there's "now" and there's "ever." Nothing between. Build the stack — week, month, season, journey — as a coherent system, not four separate features.
