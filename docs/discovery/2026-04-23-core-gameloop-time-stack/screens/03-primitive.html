The Lagerfeuer IS the time-stack

The scene itself accumulates. Weekly: a small mark on the world (a stone added, a sapling). Monthly: a Freund permanently joins the camp. Season: scenery shifts (snow, blossoms). Journey: the place is rich with memory.

Leans on: CampfireScene exists; Visitors/expedition stubs ready; painterly art language is already the brand. The world grows quietly without demanding attention.

Form: spatial/ambient. The PLACE holds memory. Nothing announces itself.

The honest tradeoffs

Quick read

  • Buch — best for parents + grandparents (the Oma/Opa digest falls out cheap). Strongest for "graduated" Wave 3 keepsake.
  • Ronki — best for kid emotional bond. Strongest for the "fades by design" arc — Ronki must be the one who says goodbye.
  • Lagerfeuer — best for ambient discovery (no FOMO, no announce). Strongest for kids who don't read yet.

Risks

  • Buch — risks feeling "documentary" / homework-y. Needs kid-pull, not just parent-pull.
  • Ronki — risks anthropomorphism overload + voice-line burden. Voice-bank is mid-rebuild.
  • Lagerfeuer — risks being TOO quiet — kids might not notice the changes; needs occasional "look what happened" moments without breaking the no-FOMO rule.
