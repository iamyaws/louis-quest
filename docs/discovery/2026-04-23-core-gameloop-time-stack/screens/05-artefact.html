Q4 locked B+C fusion: the scene remembers, Ronki narrates. This question picks the form of the memory. Two axes underneath: countable (kid can point at "this one = Tuesday") vs cumulative (the whole scene got richer; no single unit stands out).

Steine — cairn around the fire

Metapher: "Eine Erinnerung pro Woche, greifbar." Kid can tap a stone and Ronki tells the story. Each stone named and unique.

Scaling: Week = +1 stone. Month = a bigger stone (or the fire pit expands). Season = moss grows on older stones. Journey = the cairn becomes a small stone monument.

Pros: kid-direct, storytelling-strong, production-light (one shape, palette variants). Cons: saturates fast (52 stones after a year = crowded).

Pflanzen — sapling, tree, forest

Metapher: "Etwas wächst mit dir." Living growth. Each week adds a new sapling; existing saplings mature over weeks. After months: a small forest around the camp.

Scale: Week = +1 sapling. Month = early saplings become small trees. Season = scenery shift (blossoms, autumn). Journey = a forest the kid walked into.

Pros: alive, organic, season-aware for free. Strongest "growing up" metaphor. Cons: more art states per object.

LATERNEN · LICHTERKETTE
🔥
4 Laternen · Woche 4

Laternen — string of paper lanterns

Metapher: "Jede Woche ein Licht." Ceremonial, warm, parental-friendly. A string of lanterns above the camp, new lantern appears each week in a new color.

Scale: Week = +1 lantern. Month = a bigger paper moon. Season = lantern festival variant (spring blossoms, winter stars). Journey = sky full of lights.

Pros: instantly ceremonial, parent-readable, warm. Cons: less kid-handleable than stones; very "event-y" — may read as festival, not everyday.

Mix — one form per horizon

Metapher: "Jede Zeitebene hat ihre eigene Form." Stones for weeks (countable), a tree for each month (growing), scenery shift for each season (cumulative).

Scale: Explicit — every horizon has its own visual grammar. Journey = the whole camp settles into a place (tent, worn path, heirloom cairn).

Pros: each horizon visually distinct, maximum semantic density. Cons: 3× the art production, risks feeling busy vs "one quiet world."

The axis you're really picking

Countable (Steine, Laternen) reads as kid-direct storytelling — "this one is for Tuesday." Cumulative (Pflanzen) reads as fades-by-design — "the whole place grew, you can't point at any one day." Mix gets both but pays 3× in art. The pick defines how Ronki's voice works: countable = "dieser Stein ist für Dienstag," cumulative = "sieh wie deine Birke gewachsen ist seit Ostern."
