# Impeccable Design Context — Ronki

## Design Context

### Users
German parents (primarily mothers) of children ages 5–9. They are NOT tech-savvy or early adopters. They are skeptical of "another kids app" and especially sensitive to:
- Privacy and DSGVO compliance
- Manipulative design (streaks, dark patterns, aggressive notifications)
- Screen time and its effect on their kids
- Unfinished or beta-feeling products around children

They consume the marketing site during stressful moments — typically between the morning routine battle ("zehnmal Zähne putzen rufen") and the evening collapse. They need to feel calm, not sold to.

**Job to be done:** find out if this app is trustworthy, safe, and actually different from the Robloxes and Fortnites their kid already encounters at school.

### Brand Personality
Three words: **calm, honest, parent-respecting**.

Voice rules:
- Speaks parent-to-parent, never founder-to-customer
- No startup jargon ("disrupt", "seamless", "supercharge")
- No em-dashes anywhere — periods, colons, semicolons, commas only
- Short sentences. Fragments OK. "Niemand muss rufen."
- German first. English content is rare.
- Acknowledges the mess of real parenting without making parents feel judged

Emotional goals: relief, gentle trust, a quiet smile. Never urgency, never hype.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Painterly warmth** — watercolor washes, brushstroke textures, soft edges.

Palette (current, locked in):
- `teal-dark #1A3C3F` — hero background, primary text on light
- `teal #2D5A5E` — mid-weight structure
- `sage #50a082` — success states, accent
- `mustard #FCD34D` — primary highlight, CTA backgrounds
- `cream #FDF8F0` — default page background, light text on dark
- `ink` — body text

Mode: **predominantly light (cream)** with **deliberate dark hero** (teal-dark). The darkness is stability, not moodiness.

**References we're aligned with:**
- Studio Ghibli softness in character illustrations
- Gentle children's book editorial (Éditions du Seuil, not Disney)
- Warm editorial magazines (Kinfolk palette, not Apartamento)

**Anti-references** (what it must NOT look like):
- Generic SaaS with gradient blobs on white
- Bright primary-color kid app (Duolingo, Khan Academy Kids)
- Dark glowing cyan-on-navy AI aesthetic
- Flat minimalism stripped of personality
- Instagram-startup pastel-gradient-on-serif

### Design Principles
1. **Honesty visible in pixels.** Fake testimonials removed. AI art credited. No countdown timers. No streak mechanics on the marketing site itself. The design must embody the product promise.
2. **Painterly, not clinical.** Watercolor washes and hand-painted character art are core. Flat UI elements (cards, buttons) should feel soft-edged and textured, not Figma-default.
3. **Narrative over feature grid.** The storyboard section (Morgen / Nachmittag / Abend) replaces a traditional features grid. The story IS the feature list.
4. **Show the product working.** The routine cards in the storyboard section mock the real app's task list. Parents see exactly how Ronki behaves in their mornings.
5. **Every decision defends the child.** Color contrast prioritized for adult parents reading at 6am. No flash animations. Reduced-motion respected globally.

### Theme Selection Rationale
- Marketing site visited during stressful mornings/evenings → **light cream default**
- Hero section goes dark teal to feel grounded and confident, not to look "cool"
- Legal pages stay light for maximum readability during skeptical reading
